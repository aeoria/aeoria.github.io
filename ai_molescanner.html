Skin Conditions We Analyze

High Risk

Melanoma

The most serious type of skin cancer. It often starts as a new mole or a change in an existing one.

  • Asymmetrical shape
  • Irregular, blurred borders
  • Multiple colors or shades
  • Diameter larger than 6mm
High Risk

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common form of skin cancer. Usually appears as a shiny bump or a sore that doesn't heal.

  • Pearly or waxy bump
  • Flat, flesh-colored scar-like lesion
  • Bleeding or scabbing sore
High Risk

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Develops in the squamous cells that make up the middle and outer layers of the skin.

  • Firm, red nodule
  • Flat lesion with a scaly, crusted surface
  • New sore or raised area on an old scar
Low Risk

Benign Moles (Nevi)

Common skin growths that are usually harmless but should be monitored for changes over time.

  • Uniform brown or tan color
  • Distinct, round edges
  • Stable size and shape
